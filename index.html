Portfolio

Virginia Alcohol Dashboard

At Counter Tools, we built a data dashboard using Tableau for Virginia Department of Behavioral Health and Developmental Services to share insights on alcohol control in the state. Working with the client's Project Director and our Data Manager, I designed the dashboard layout in line with the client's branding and helped pull the pieces together. Users can select their local geographic area from a dropdown and see statistics populate across all charts and maps, read contextual research on the effects of alcohol, and gain a greater understanding on issues affecting their community.

This project was a new service offering for our team and required a deep dive into learning the tool quickly.

Courses

In 2022 I completed the Web Accessibility Specialist Curriculum through Deque University, which covered accessibility fundamentals and then dove deep into accessibility design for web. I learned a lot about how assistive devices are actually used and gained a greater understanding of how to design more inclusively.

I was excited to be accepted into the Grow With Google Developer Scholarship in 2018. Our community of 25,000 aspiring developers learned HTML, CSS, and Javascript.

After this inital challenge period, 5,000 students were selected for the full Nanodegree scholarship in Front End Web Development. This course had more in-depth study of Javascript libraries including Ember, Angular, and React, and included accessibility best practices.

I was also lucky to be accepted into the Bertelsmann Data Science Scholarship in 2018. This community of 15,000 students took an Intro to Descriptive Statistics course where we studied data analysis, python, and SQL.

After completion I enrolled in the Business Analytics Nanodegree which built on these skills with projects featuring data visualization and data dashboards using Excel and Tableau.
